Payment Options

Maalwala doesn't process payments — it's a listing platform. Freight is paid directly between the shipper and transporter, however they agree. Here's how most users on load boards like this typically settle up, and a few safety habits worth following.

UPI

Fastest for advances and part-payments. Confirm the receiver's UPI ID or scan their QR code directly — don't trust a screenshot forwarded by a third party.

Bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS/IMPS)

Common for larger freight amounts. Ask for a cancelled cheque or bank proof before sending an advance to a new contact.

Cash

Still common for smaller local loads. Always get a signed receipt (a builty/LR — Lorry Receipt — counts) noting the amount and date.

Cheque

Used for balance payments with regular business partners. Confirm the account is active before relying on it for time-sensitive payments.

Advance + balance on delivery

The most common structure: a percentage upfront (often 20-30%) to confirm the booking, and the balance on POD (proof of delivery).

A few safety habits

Get the rate, advance amount, and payment terms agreed in writing (even a WhatsApp message) before the truck loads. Verify the other party's GST number and, for larger loads, their vehicle RC. Always generate the e-way bill correctly. Never share OTPs with anyone, including someone claiming to be from Maalwala — we will never ask for one.

Recharge FASTag

Maalwala doesn't process FASTag recharges directly — we're not a licensed payment processor, and we'd rather send you somewhere trustworthy than build something half-safe. Below are the real, official ways to recharge, and a couple of safety notes worth knowing.

Your issuing bank

The most reliable option. FASTag is issued by your bank (ICICI, HDFC, SBI, Axis, Paytm Payments Bank, and 20+ others) — recharge through their official app or net banking, the same way you'd pay any bill.

My FASTag app (IHMCL)

The official app from IHMCL (the NHAI-promoted body running the FASTag programme). Works via UPI, net banking, or card, regardless of which bank issued your tag. Available on the Play Store; iOS availability may vary.

IHMCL official portal

For NHAI-issued (bank-neutral) FASTags specifically, or to check KYC status and account details.

Open ihmcl.co.in →

UPI apps

Paytm, PhonePe, and Google Pay all support FASTag recharge as a bill category — search "FASTag" inside the app and enter your vehicle number.

Good to know

Minimum recharge is usually ₹100, maximum ₹1,00,000 per transaction. Always confirm you're on your bank's real app or official website — FASTag recharge is a common scam target. Never share an OTP, and never pay through a link sent to you by someone claiming to be "FASTag support." NHAI's helpline for FASTag issues is 1033.
